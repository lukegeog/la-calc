Clinical reminders

Doses used

  • Lidocaine plain: 3 mg/kg.
  • Lidocaine with adrenaline: 7 mg/kg.
  • Bupivacaine plain: 2 mg/kg.
  • Bupivacaine with adrenaline: 3 mg/kg (local trust protocol; BNF is more conservative at 2 mg/kg).
  • Adrenaline 1:200,000 = 5 µg/ml. Conventional max total 250 µg — shown for awareness on each row but not enforced by the calculator.
  • No caps applied. The calculator outputs pure mg/kg dosing. No absolute mg ceiling and no adrenaline ceiling — keep an eye on the µg total yourself.

Adjustments

  • Reduce by 30–50% in elderly, frail, hepatic impairment, or low cardiac output.
  • Use ideal body weight in obesity.
  • Avoid adrenaline in fingers, toes, ears, nose, penis (classic teaching — the literature is now more permissive but follow your unit’s practice).

LA toxicity

Tinnitus → perioral numbness → metallic taste → agitation → seizures → cardiac arrhythmia → arrest. Stop injecting; call for help; airway / oxygen / circulation; for cardiac signs give 20% lipid emulsion 1.5 ml/kg bolus, then infusion 0.25 ml/kg/min (max cumulative ~12 ml/kg). AAGBI guideline on every theatre wall.
